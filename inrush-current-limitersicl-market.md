<p><p>Inrush Current Limiters (ICLs) are devices designed to restrict high transient currents during equipment start-up, protecting circuits from damage. The market comprises primarily two types: NTC (Negative Temperature Coefficient) ICLs, which decrease resistance as they heat up, effectively limiting current during initial power-up but allowing normal operation thereafter; and PTC (Positive Temperature Coefficient) ICLs, which increase resistance when overheating occurs, protecting the circuit from sustained overcurrents. Additionally, other specialized ICLs cater to unique applications across various industries.</p></p>
